DRIVING TIPS

Master The Roads of The UAE with Al-Baz


Drive Smart & Safe

1. Know the Road Rules:

The UAE follows strict traffic regulations. Always drive on the right-hand side and follow posted speed limits, which are clearly marked in kilometers per hour.

2. Speed Cameras Are Everywhere:

Speed limits are enforced by radar cameras across all Emirates. Exceeding limits can result in heavy fines, so stay alert and within the legal speed.

3. Use Indicators and Avoid Lane Swerving:

Lane discipline is strictly monitored. Always use your indicator when changing lanes or turning, and avoid weaving through traffic.

4. Never Use Your Phone While Driving:

Using a mobile phone without a hands-free device is illegal and dangerous. Use Bluetooth or voice commands if you must take a call.

5. Respect Parking Rules

Unauthorized parking in restricted or disabled zones can result in hefty fines or towing. Always park in designated areas.

6. Beware of Sudden Fog & Sandstorms:

Drive with caution during weather changes. Use low-beam headlights in fog, and never stop on the road. Pull over to a safe spot if visibility drops.

7. Children Must Use Car Seats:

By law, children under 10 years old must use a child seat and are not allowed in the front seat of the car.

8. Check Salik & Tolls:

Dubai uses an automatic toll system (Salik). Ensure your rental car has a registered tag, and understand how charges apply during your trip.

9. Drive Defensively, Not Aggressively:

Stay calm even if others drive fast or unpredictably. Defensive driving is the key to safety on UAE highways.

10. Emergency Numbers to Remember
  • 999 – Police
  • 998 – Ambulance
  • 997 – Fire Department
Keep these saved in case of any roadside emergencies.
